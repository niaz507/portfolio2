Case study

Roti Wala — A website that orders as easily as it reads

Roti Wala Snack House & Café serves authentic Bangladeshi street food on Kingsway, Vancouver. Their old site was a single text-heavy page that buried the menu, the halal assurance, and the ordering links — for an audience that is 72% mobile. This redesign fixed that with research, not guesswork.

Role
UI/UX Designer
Type
Website Redesign
Client
Roti Wala, Vancouver
Deliverables
Research + responsive design

Research & the problem

Analytics showed 2,800 monthly sessions with a 72% mobile / 23% desktop split, driven mostly by social media and word of mouth. A survey of 60 Bangladeshi customers in Vancouver revealed what people want first: menu and prices (78%), location and hours (67%), and halal confirmation (55%). The existing site offered none of these quickly — a text-heavy hero, no dietary tags, and delivery links hidden from view.

The redesign

A mobile-first layout puts the essentials above the fold: a clear halal badge, vegetarian options, one strong "Order Now" call to action, and real photography of the food. Signature dishes like Kacchi Biryani and Beef Tehari get short English descriptions so both the Bangladeshi community and curious food lovers can decide what to eat fast. Warm colors inspired by tea, biryani, and terracotta celebrate the culture in a clean, modern way.

A clear path to order

The ordering section presents Uber Eats and DoorDash side by side with prominent buttons, alongside location, hours, and phone — so every visitor type, from students ordering after class to families planning dinner, reaches their goal in one or two taps.
